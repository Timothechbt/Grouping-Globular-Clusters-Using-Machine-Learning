# Grouping Globular Clusters Using Machine Learning

Welcome to our repo!

In the current paradigm of galaxy formation, galaxies grow through a hierarchical process involving the accretion of lower-mass galaxies. The Milky Way is no exception: it has been built over time by incorporating a number of galaxies. Some of these accretions are ongoing, and their corresponding structures are still recognizable. Others, however, are ancient: the accreted systems have long since dissolved and their stars dispersed into the galactic halo, making them difficult to identify. Among the notable components of accreted galaxies are globular clusters. These dense, ancient systems may have survived the dissolution of their host galaxies, but they have also lost their group coherence. Reconstructing groups of globular clusters originating from the same progenitor galaxy would allow us to trace the history of accretion episodes and improve our understanding of the Milky Way's formation.

Spatial, kinematic, and dynamic information (positions, velocities, and orbits) are not enough to group clusters of a common origin. On the other hand, using the chemical composition of the stars within the clusters offers a promising lead. Clusters from the same galaxy should share similar chemical signatures, inherited from the chemical evolution of their galaxy of origin. 

The objective of this project is to explore the use of artificial intelligence methods to identify groups of globular clusters sharing a common origin, based on chemical abundances measured by the APOGEE spectroscopic survey.
